Project Brief

History

Pakistan has a long history of environmental planning and management however it was the launch of its National Conservation Strategy (NCS) in 1992 that marked the beginning of its recent sustainability journey. Highlighting the importance of environmental information and its integration in broader economic development, NCS lead to the creation of a first set of national and provincial institutions that would carry this responsibility.

Environmental Information Management System

Since then, several projects have been undertaken for the purpose. While significant environmental capacity and awareness have been created, sustainable Environmental Information Management System and integration of environmental and development remain to be adequately achieved. This project would help achieve this by adressing the barriers to the following key outcomes.

Regular availability of consistent and reliable environmental data.

Impact

Upto date information

Pakistan is home to numerous ecosystems and species, upto date information about them and the country's biodiversity is seldom available due to lack of resources. The available info is old and not accessible easily. GEB's EIMS will make sure upto date and reliable information is available.

Early Warning Systems

The country's glacial melts feeds the Indus river system that makes the Indus River Basin as one of the world's most fertile and productive area in what would otherwise be a desert with low and erratic rainfall. Pakistan's aggrarian economy depends on this river system. There's also a recurrent threat of frequent high intensity floods attributed in part to climate change which damages formlands, irrigation infrastructure and means of communication. GEB's EIMS can be utilized particularly in the Risk analysis phase of early warning systems for such threats.

Environmental Management

A successful implementation of this project in an environment consisting of such a rich and complex ecosystem and institutions, paralleled by high density population wouldn't only be benefitial for Pakistan, but the experience and the knowledge attained from the project will contribute to the global knowledge and capacity for environmental management.
