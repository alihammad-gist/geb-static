Enhanced decision making at the federal and local level, through the use of Environmental Management Systems and Environmental knowledge . National Capacity Self Assesment for Pakistan highlighted the lack of Capacties , Infrastructure , and adequate technology to meet its obligations under the following convention

- Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD)
- United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC)
- United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification (UNCCD)

Lack of Operational, Infrastructural capacities and low level of training as key capacity gaps for CBD implementation. Weak capacities for climate related forecasts and early warning systems as major implementation impediments for UNFCCC. Capacity gaps in dedicated and coordinated forest information system / GIS (Geographical Information System) based forest resource accounting and monitoring system to asses the extent and impact of desertification.

This project is a joint initiative of Government of Pakistan, United Nations Development Program, and Global Environment Facility.

Outcomes

Upto date information

Pakistan is home to numerous ecosystems and species, upto date information about them and the country's biodiversity is seldom available due to lack of resources. The available info is old and not accessible easily. GEB's EIMS will make sure upto date and reliable information is available.

Early Warning Systems

Pakistan's aggrarian economy depends on this river system. There's also a recurrent threat of frequent high intensity floods attributed in part to climate change which damages formlands, irrigation infrastructure and means of communication. GEB's EIMS can be utilized particularly in the Risk analysis phase of early warning systems for such threats.

Environmental Management

A successful implementation of this project in an environment consisting of such a rich and complex ecosystem and institutions, paralleled by high density population wouldn't only be benefitial for Pakistan, but the experience and the knowledge attained from the project will contribute to the global knowledge and capacity for environmental management.
